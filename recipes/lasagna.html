Description

Japanese curry (karē) is a deeply comforting dish characterized by its thick, glossy, and mildly sweet sauce. Unlike Indian or Thai curries, it is typically made using a dark, store-bought roux block, simmered with tender chunks of meat, potatoes, and carrots, and served generously over short-grain Japanese rice.
